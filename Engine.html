During my second year of studies, I worked in a group project. We had four months to come up with an idea and create a prototype for a world war two bomber game.

Specifically I worked on the design and look of the game. I created the model for the main bomber and the game environment including lighting and the aestethic. We created the game in Unity. The game is in a very basic stage however, we were invited to the university showcase where the group won "Best Military Exhibit" out of the entire showcase.

A personal project of mine made in unity. It is a 3D rendering of a city that I have been using in my D&D campaigns however I have slowly begun adding functionality to turn this empty city into a game.

The state of the game currently is a very simple prototype. I am however, still working on this and improving it.

Another personal project made in Unity, this time in 2D. The game is in a basic prototype stage with placeholder art. The idea comes from the game Divekick which has a one hit to win mechanic.

The game is in a simple stage with no AI for the enemy model yet. The game works like a standard fighting game, however it only take two hits to win or lose and the setting is gothic sci-fi (think warhammer 40k).
